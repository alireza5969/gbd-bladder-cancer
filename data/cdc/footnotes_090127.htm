Vulnerability Disclosure Policy

How should this data set be cited?

Suggested Citation

Centers for Disease Control and Prevention. Environmental Public Health Tracking Network. Pesticide Exposures. Accessed From: https://ephtracking.cdc.gov/DataExplorer. Accessed on 05/15/2025

Why was the data set created?

Purpose

Poison Control Center data for pesticides is best used to estimate exposures that occur in residential environments.

How was the data set created?

Derivation of measure

Reported pesticide exposures measures will be developed from self-reported data provided by the American Association of Poison Control Centers (AAPCC) collected from the Poison Control Centers in the U.S.

What are the limitations of the data set?

Exposures to pesticides are self-reported unless very serious and exposure requires hospitalization or results in a major health outcome treated by a physician or emergency department. Pesticide poisonings are likely underreported because of difficulty with diagnosis, incomplete reporting to surveillance systems, (Calvert, et al, 2004) symptoms not being recognized as a poisoning (Texas Department of State Health Services, 2010). Chronic illnesses from long-term low-dose exposures, which may be more typical of pesticides exposures, are not included in poison control center data. State-specific data has various fine distinctions from state to state. Utilization of PCs per unit population varies widely across the U.S., so it is very hard to compare between states. For example, just because CA sees more pesticide exposure calls compared to GA does not necessarily mean CA has more pesticide exposures; it could be due to the fact that PCs are called more in CA. Another limitation is that poison center practices vary by poison center, so their coding of reported exposures may be different; this will be noted in the footnotes section on the national portal noted as 'About These Data'. Differences can often be seen in the coding of the data field 'Exposure Reason'.

How should these data be used?

Use Constraints

These are self-reported exposures to pesticides and as such are subject to significant underreporting. In addition, because these exposures are self-reported the type of pesticide may be mis-classified since the designation by the poison control center is based on the information provided by the caller.
